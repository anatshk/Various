Greek PM says ball now in creditors' court to clinch deal
Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras arrives to attend a Eurozone emergency summit on Greece in Brussels, Belgium June 22, 2015. REUTERS/Charles Platiau
BRUSSELS Greek Prime Minister Alexis Tsipras on Monday reiterated that his government was seeking a deal with creditors that would resolve the country's financing needs, saying the ball was now in the court of European lenders to clinch one.
Tsipras made the comments after a meeting of euro zone leaders that signaled progress toward reaching a deal to avert a Greek bankruptcy after his government made a series of concessions aimed at hitting budget targets&$&.We are seeking a comprehensive and viable solution which will be followed by a strong growth package and at the same time render the Greek economy viable," he told reporters&$&.The criteria are economic viability, a sustainable debt and a strong, front-loaded growth package&$&.
